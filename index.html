About AllergyLocate

I built this because finding allergen information before eating somewhere shouldn't require 20 minutes of Googling. Most major restaurants already publish allergen PDFs or pages — they're just hard to find. AllergyLocate indexes links to that information in one place.

I'm Zamin Rizvi, a high school student with severe food allergies. I built this in 2026. All links were verified manually by me and a team of researchers.

AllergyLocate is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by any restaurant listed. We are an independent, referential directory. Allergen information is collected from publicly available sources and user submissions.

NJ Senate Bill S3394

This site exists partly because no law currently requires restaurants to post allergen information publicly. NJ S3394 would change that — requiring all NJ restaurants to publicly disclose allergen info for menu items.

What this database shows is a split picture: large national chains have largely figured out allergen disclosure. Independent restaurants — including every independent NJ restaurant we checked — have not. That gap is exactly what S3394 would close. The bill isn't asking restaurants to do something unprecedented. It's asking them to do what the major chains already do.

The 9 major food allergens (US)

Per the FDA: milk, eggs, fish, shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soybeans, and sesame. Restaurants in this database publish information about some or all of these. Most disclosures are at the category level — for example, "tree nuts" rather than specific nuts like hazelnut or almond. Always confirm your specific allergen with the restaurant directly.

Important limitations

  • Allergen information may be incomplete, outdated, or vary by location.
  • Cross-contact is always possible even when a dish is listed as allergen-free.
  • We link to publicly available information — we do not verify its medical accuracy.
  • This site does not provide medical advice. Always consult your doctor or allergist for guidance on managing your allergies.
  • Always confirm allergen information directly with the restaurant before ordering.
